Topic: は (wa) - Topic Marker
は is used to mark the topic of a sentence. It tells the listener what the sentence is about.
Example: 私は学生です。(Watashi wa gakusei desu.) - I am a student.
Note: は is written as "ha" in romaji but pronounced "wa" when used as a particle.

Topic: が (ga) - Subject Marker
が marks the grammatical subject of a sentence. It emphasizes who is doing the action.
Example: 猫がいます。(Neko ga imasu.) - There is a cat.
Note: は vs が is one of the most common points of confusion for beginners.

Topic: 食べる (taberu) - To Eat
食べる is a Group 2 verb meaning "to eat".
Example: 私はすしを食べる。(Watashi wa sushi wo taberu.) - I eat sushi.
Conjugations: 食べます (polite), 食べない (negative), 食べた (past)

Topic: ている (te iru) - Ongoing Action
ている expresses an action currently in progress, similar to "-ing" in English.
Example: 食べています。(Tabete imasu.) - I am eating.
Note: In casual speech, ている is often shortened to てる (teru).

Topic: 日 (nichi/hi) - Day/Sun
日 is one of the most common kanji. It means "day" or "sun".
Readings: にち (nichi), じつ (jitsu), ひ (hi)
Example: 今日は何曜日ですか。(Kyou wa nan youbi desu ka.) - What day is it today?
Note: 日 appears in many time-related words like 日曜日 (nichiyoubi - Sunday), 毎日 (mainichi - every day).

Topic: です (desu) - Copula
です is the polite form of the copula, similar to "is/am/are" in English.
Example: 私は学生です。(Watashi wa gakusei desu.) - I am a student.
Note: In casual speech, です is often dropped or replaced with だ (da).

Topic: 行く (iku) - To Go
行く is a Group 1 verb meaning "to go".
Example: 学校に行く。(Gakkou ni iku.) - I go to school.
Conjugations: 行きます (ikimasu - polite), 行かない (ikanai - negative), 行った (itta - past)
Note: The past tense is irregular: 行った (itta), not 行いた.

Topic: たい (tai) - Want to do
たい is attached to the verb stem to express desire or wanting to do something.
Example: 日本に行きたい。(Nihon ni ikitai.) - I want to go to Japan.
Note: たい conjugates like an i-adjective: 行きたくない (ikitakunai - don't want to go).

Topic: てください (te kudasai) - Please do
てください is used to make polite requests.
Example: 見てください。(Mite kudasai.) - Please look.
Note: For negative requests, use ないでください (naide kudasai): 触らないでください (sawaranaide kudasai - Please don't touch).

Topic: ようにする (you ni suru) - To try to / Make effort to
ようにする expresses making an effort or habit of doing something.
Example: 毎日運動するようにしている。(Mainichi undou suru you ni shite iru.) - I try to exercise every day.
Note: Different from ようになる (you ni naru) which means "to come to be able to" or a change in state.

Topic: 届ける (todokeru) - To deliver
届ける is a Group 2 verb meaning "to deliver" or "to report".
Example: 荷物を届けます。(Nimotsu wo todokemasu.) - I will deliver the package.
Note: 届く (todoku) is the intransitive form meaning "to arrive" or "to reach".
