Topic: は (wa) - Topic Marker
は is used to mark the topic of a sentence. It tells the listener what the sentence is about.
Example: 私は学生です。(Watashi wa gakusei desu.) - I am a student.
Note: は is written as "ha" in romaji but pronounced "wa" when used as a particle.

Topic: が (ga) - Subject Marker
が marks the grammatical subject of a sentence. It emphasizes who is doing the action.
Example: 猫がいます。(Neko ga imasu.) - There is a cat.
Note: は vs が is one of the most common points of confusion for beginners.

Topic: 食べる (taberu) - To Eat
食べる is a Group 2 verb meaning "to eat".
Example: 私はすしを食べる。(Watashi wa sushi wo taberu.) - I eat sushi.
Conjugations: 食べます (polite), 食べない (negative), 食べた (past)

Topic: ている (te iru) - Ongoing Action
ている expresses an action currently in progress, similar to "-ing" in English.
Example: 食べています。(Tabete imasu.) - I am eating.
Note: In casual speech, ている is often shortened to てる (teru).
